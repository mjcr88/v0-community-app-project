## Voice & Personality

### **The Ecovilla Voice**

Ecovilla sounds like a **warm neighbor who's really good at organizing things**. We're:
- **70% Warm & Welcoming** - Friendly, approachable, personal
- **30% Helpful Guide** - Clear, supportive, practical
- **+10% Playful** - Light moments with R√≠o (context-appropriate)

**Not**: Corporate, stuffy, overly formal, pushy, or treating users like children

## Core Principles

### **1. Context-Aware Copy**

Copy adapts based on:
- **What screen?** (Login vs Dashboard vs Error)
- **What emotion?** (Stressed, excited, confused, calm)
- **Which persona?** (Sofia, Marcus, Elena, Carmen)
- **What moment?** (First time, returning, celebrating, struggling)

**Example - Same Action, Different Contexts:**

| Context | Copy |
|---------|------|
| **First event created** | "Your first event! üéâ R√≠o is excited to see the community gather" |
| **Regular event created** | "Event created! Your neighbors will see it on the calendar" |
| **Error creating event** | "Couldn't create the event. Check your connection and try again?" |

---

### **2. Community-Focused Language**

We weave community naturally into copy without forcing it:

**‚úÖ Do:**
- "Your neighbors are gathering tomorrow"
- "3 residents attending"
- "Share with your community"
- "Welcome to Ecovilla San Mateo"

**‚ùå Don't:**
- "Your community members are gathering" (awkward)
- "Community residents attending" (redundant)
- "Share with your Ecovilla community members" (wordy)
- "Welcome to the Ecovilla San Mateo intentional community platform" (exhausting)

**Use:**
- "Neighbors" over "users" or "members"
- "Residents" over "accounts" or "profiles"
- "Community" naturally, not forcefully
- "Gathering" or "get-together" over "meeting"

---

### **3. User-Focused Voice**

**Primary:** You/Your (user-focused)  
**Secondary:** We/Us (partnership moments)  
**Avoid:** Platform-centric or third-person

**‚úÖ Examples:**
- "Your events" (ownership)
- "You have 3 new announcements" (direct)
- "We're here to help" (partnership, rare)
- "Let's get you set up" (onboarding, rare)

**‚ùå Avoid:**
- "User events" (technical)
- "The system has 3 announcements" (robotic)
- "Platform functionality includes..." (corporate)

## Copy Patterns by Type

### **Headings**

**Page Titles:**
- Clear, direct nouns: "Events", "Dashboard", "Map"
- Never questions: Not "What's happening?"
- Context when helpful: "Your Profile", "Community Events"

**Section Headings:**
- Action-oriented when appropriate: "Upcoming Gatherings"
- Descriptive: "Recent Announcements", "Active Residents"
- Keep short: 2-4 words ideal

**Empty State Headings:**
- Simple statement: "No events yet"
- Avoid negativity: Not "You have no friends" (use "No connections yet")
- Sometimes playful: "The calendar is lonely"

---

### **Buttons & CTAs**

**Formula:** [Verb] + [Benefit/Object]

**Primary Actions:**
- "Create Event" (direct)
- "Join Gathering" (inviting)
- "Connect with Neighbors" (benefit-oriented)
- "Start Building Your Profile" (personal)
- "Explore the Community" (inviting)

**Secondary Actions:**
- "Learn More"
- "Maybe Later"
- "Skip for Now"
- "Go Back"

**Destructive Actions:**
- "Yes, Delete" (confirm explicitly)
- "Remove Event" (clear consequence)
- Never just "OK" for destructive actions

---

### **Success Messages**

**Formula:** [Celebration] + [Confirmation] + [Optional: Next Step]

**Big Wins (Celebratory + Next Step):**
- "Event created! üéâ Your neighbors will see it on the calendar"
- "Profile complete! ‚ú® Ready to explore your community?"
- "First check-in! Nice! Your neighbors know where to find you"

**Regular Actions (Confirmational + Next Step):**
- "Event updated. Changes are live"
- "Request submitted. We'll notify you when it's reviewed"
- "Profile saved. Looking good!"

**Minor Actions (Simple Confirmation):**
- "Saved"
- "Done"
- "Updated"

**Toast Duration:**
- Celebratory: 4 seconds (time to read and feel good)
- Regular: 3 seconds
- Minor: 2 seconds

---

### **Error Messages**

**Formula:** [What Happened] + [Why/Context] + [Solution]

**Network Errors:**
- "Couldn't save your changes. Check your connection and try again?"
- "Can't load events right now. Are you online?"

**Validation Errors:**
- "That email is already in use. Want to log in instead?"
- "Please enter a valid email address"
- "Event needs a title and date to continue"

**Permission Errors:**
- "You don't have permission to do that. Contact your admin?"
- "This content isn't available to you"

**Generic Errors:**
- "Hmm, that didn't work. Try again?"
- "Something went wrong. We're looking into it"

**Never:**
- Technical jargon: "Error 500", "Null pointer exception"
- Blame users: "You entered invalid data"
- Dead ends: "Error occurred" with no solution
- ALL CAPS: "ERROR!"

### **Loading States**

**Short Waits (< 2 seconds):**
- Spinner only, no text
- Or: Simple present tense: "Loading..."

**Medium Waits (2-5 seconds):**
- "Loading your events..."
- "Getting things ready..."
- "Checking with the community..."

**Long Waits (> 5 seconds):**
- R√≠o animation + text
- "R√≠o is gathering information..."
- "This might take a moment..."
- "Almost there..."

**Never:**
- Percentages (unless truly accurate): Not "Loading 47%"
- Time estimates if uncertain: Not "30 seconds remaining" (unless sure)
- Anxiety-inducing: Not "Please wait, do not close window"

---

### **Confirmations**

**Destructive Actions:**
- "Delete this event? This can't be undone."
- "Remove Sofia from your family? You can add her back later."
- "Leave this gathering? Neighbors are counting on you."

**Non-Destructive:**
- "Leave without saving changes?"
- "Cancel this request? You can create a new one anytime."

**Buttons:**
- Destructive: "Yes, Delete" + "Cancel" (default)
- Non-destructive: "Yes, Leave" + "Keep Editing" (default)

---

### **Microcopy & Helper Text**

**Form Field Helpers:**
- Be specific: "Your email for login and notifications"
- Set expectations: "Visible to all community members"
- Not obvious: Skip "Enter your name" (the label says "Name")

**Tooltips:**
- Brief: 5-10 words max
- Actually helpful: Not "This is a button"
- Contextual: "This will notify all residents"

**Placeholders:**
- Examples: "your@email.com" not "Email address"
- Helpful: "e.g., Yoga in the garden"
- Never instructions: Not "Type your password here"

**Character Counts:**
- Positive: "23 characters remaining"
- Not negative: Not "523 characters over limit" (show at 0)

## Writing for Context

### **High-Stress Moments**

**When:** Errors, urgent requests, account issues

**Voice:** Professional, clear, calm, solution-focused

**Do:**
- Be direct
- Offer clear solutions
- Show empathy without over-apologizing
- Provide support contact

**Don't:**
- Add personality (no R√≠o, no emojis)
- Be casual or flip
- Hide behind jargon
- Blame anyone

**Example:**
```
Couldn't process your request

This might be a temporary issue. 
Try again in a few minutes?

If this keeps happening, contact support.

[Try Again] [Contact Support]
```

---

### **Celebration Moments**

**When:** Achievements, first-time actions, milestones

**Voice:** Warm, celebratory, next-step oriented

**Do:**
- Celebrate genuinely
- Use emojis
- Suggest next action

**When:** First login, feature discovery, tutorials

**Voice:** Welcoming, patient, clear, encouraging

- Welcome warmly

- Use R√≠o for personality

## Grammar & Style Rules

### **Spelling & Capitalization**

- **US English** (organization, color, neighbor)
- **Sentence case** for headings: "Your profile" not "Your Profile"
- **Title Case** only for proper nouns: "Ecovilla San Mateo"
- **Capitalize**: UI elements when referring to them: "Click the Create button"

---

### **Punctuation**

- **Periods**: Use in paragraphs, skip in single-line UI text
- **Exclamation marks**: Celebrations only (1 per message max)
- **Question marks**: When genuinely asking (not rhetorical questions)
- **Ellipsis**: Loading states only ("Loading...")
- **Colons**: Introduce lists or explanations

**Examples:**
```
‚úÖ Welcome back, Sofia! (greeting)
‚úÖ Event created (confirmation, no period)
‚úÖ Want to learn more? (genuine question)
‚úÖ Loading your events... (in progress)
‚ùå Check out the new features!!! (too many)
‚ùå You created an event. (period unnecessary)
```

---

### **Numbers**

- **Spell out**: One to nine
- **Numerals**: 10+, dates, times, counts
- **Always numerals**: Stats, badges, technical specs

**Examples:**
- "three announcements" (small)
- "15 residents attending" (larger)
- "2 hours ago" (time)
- "42 active residents" (stat)

---

### **Dates & Times**

- **Relative**: Recent actions ("2 hours ago", "yesterday")
- **Absolute**: Future events ("Nov 21, 2024", "Tomorrow at 10am")
- **Format**: Month DD, YYYY or "Tomorrow/Today"
- **Time**: 12-hour with am/pm ("2:30pm")

---

### **Contractions**

**Use freely** - Makes copy conversational:
- you're, we're, can't, didn't, it's, that's, here's, there's

**Exception**: Avoid in critical moments (security, legal)

## Copy Review Checklist

Before finalizing any copy:

**Voice & Tone:**
- [ ] Feels warm and casual (not formal or robotic)
- [ ] User-focused (you/your prominent)
- [ ] Community language feels natural
- [ ] Appropriate for context (celebrating/calm/stressed)
- [ ] Matches persona needs if applicable

**Clarity:**
- [ ] Scannable (can user understand in 2 seconds?)
- [ ] No jargon or technical terms
- [ ] Action is clear (what happens when I click?)
- [ ] No ambiguity

**Helpfulness:**
- [ ] Errors include solutions
- [ ] Empty states encourage action
- [ ] Success messages confirm what happened
- [ ] Next steps are obvious

**Consistency:**
- [ ] Button text follows verb + object pattern
- [ ] Similar actions use similar words
- [ ] Tone matches other copy on this screen
- [ ] Grammar and style rules followed

**Emojis & R√≠o:**
- [ ] Emojis used sparingly (if at all)
- [ ] R√≠o appears only in appropriate moments (5-10% of copy)
- [ ] Personality enhances, doesn't distract

**Accessibility:**
- [ ] Screen reader friendly (no "click here")
- [ ] No reliance on color/icons alone for meaning
- [ ] Error messages announced clearly

## Using This Guide

### **For Writing New Copy:**
1. Identify the context (what screen, what emotion, which persona)
2. Choose appropriate voice characteristics
3. Apply relevant copy pattern
4. Review with checklist
5. Test with someone outside the project

### **For Reviewing Existing Copy:**
1. Read through the guide
2. Compare against examples
3. Check voice & tone alignment
4. Verify clarity and helpfulness
5. Update as needed

### **For Cursor/AI Implementation:**
1. Reference this guide in prompts
2. Specify context clearly
3. Provide persona information
4. Request multiple options
5. Review and refine output
